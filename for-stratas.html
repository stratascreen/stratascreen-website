The challenge every strata faces
Strata fees go up. Reserves stay thin.
"What happens when the reserve fund looks healthy on paper — until one bad winter hits the boiler?"

Strata councils are asked to make hard decisions constantly: Which repairs can wait? How much of a fee increase will residents accept? Is the contingency reserve actually enough — or does it just feel like enough until it isn't?

The honest answer for most buildings is that there's never quite enough cushion. There's no slack in the system. One surprise eats months of reserves, and the only lever available is raising fees on residents who are already stretched.

That's the reality. And it's nobody's fault — it's just a structural problem with how strata financing works. What if your lobby could help fix that?
